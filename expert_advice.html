Health-care tip for Toddler

1. Newborn babies don’t come with a manual on how to care for and handle them.
2. We recommend the baby sleep on his or her back for the first six months.
3. Above 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit is considered a fever in infants.
4. Start with offering mashed foods that are easily digested.
5. Until twelve months cow’s milk should only be used in small amounts to mix with family foods and in cooking.
6. Avoid nutrient poor foods with high levels of saturated fat, sugar, or salt (e.g. cakes, biscuits, confectionery and potato chips).




Health-care tip for Child

1. Breakfast is the most important meal of the day.
2. Teach them to wash their hands often.
3. Exercise is essential as well.
4. Sleep is important for your child.
5. Check with your doctor and get a timely schedule vaccination according to your child’s age, health and vaccines available.
6. Encourage your child to participate and engage in activities other than sitting in front of the PC and TV.




Health-care tip for Teenager

1. Take a BATH daily.
2. Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables, whole grains, a variety of protein foods, and low-fat dairy products.
3. Teenagers with obesity are more likely to have obesity as an adult. They are also at higher risk for other chronic illnesses, depression, and bullying.
4. Sleep has a strong effect on your ability to concentrate and do well at school.
5. You can’t avoid stress, so you need to learn how to manage it.
6. Limit your activities to the most important ones and give those 100%. Overextending yourself can lead to stress, frustration, or exhaustion.




Health-care tip for Youngster

1. Pay attention to your moods and feelings.
2. If you feel like you have low self-esteem or a poor body image, talk to someone about it. Even just talking to a friend can help.
3. Avoid substance use or abuse. This includes alcohol, street drugs, other people’s prescription drugs, and any type of tobacco product.
4. Wear a helmet when you are riding a bike or participating in sports to prevent concussions. Concussions at a young age can have lifelong negative effects on your health.
5. Too Much Caffeine Can Make You Fat
6. Build physical activity into your daily life. Find a way to get 20 or 30 minutes of activity each day, including riding a bike or briskly walking to work.




Health-care tip for Adult

1. You Have to Exercise Your Brain.
2. You Need An Emergency Health Fund.
3. Too Much Tech Can Harm Your Health.
4. At this age, vision can start to worsen, so have your eyes checked out.
5. Starting at age 40, we lose about 1 percent of muscle mass per year.So people can benefit from incorporating weight-bearing exercises, along with cardiovascular exercise, into a weekly physical activity plan.
6. Wear a helmet when riding a bike.Wear a seatbelt in cars.Don’t text and drive.




Health-care tip for Senior

1. You Need Health Insurance.
2. Exercise Can Be Your Savior.
3. Some supplements are recommended include calcium, vitamin D, vitamin B6, or vitamin B12.Take supplements or multivitamins as instructed to boost your immune system.
4. You should also limit your consumption of sugary and fatty foods, which can trigger inflammation in the body and lower your immune system.
5. It’s possible to become ill if you touch a virus-covered surface and contaminate your hands, and then touch your face.
6. Scheduling a yearly checkup can also keep you healthy. Always speak with your doctor if you have concerns about your health.Conditions like diabetes and high blood pressure can go undetected.
